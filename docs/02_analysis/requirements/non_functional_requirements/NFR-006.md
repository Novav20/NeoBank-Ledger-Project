# NFR-006: Enforce Quorum-Certified Finality and Prevent Double-Spending

## Statement
The Ledger system shall only treat a block as final after quorum certification and shall prevent double-spending across conflicting histories.

## Rationale
[[BPA_Report#4.2 Consolidated NFR Registry|BPA NFR-SAFE-01]] defines the safety target as QC-enforced finality with double-spend prevention. [[berger_2023b]] describes quorum certificates and consensus finality, [[benedetti_2022]] describes deterministic finality with quorum-certificate-based block solution handling, and [[praveen_2024]] formally verifies fork-freedom and double-spend safety. This makes the safety requirement directly traceable without introducing a gap.

## Acceptance Criteria
- [ ] A block is not considered final until quorum-certification conditions are satisfied.
- [ ] The system does not accept two conflicting committed histories at the same height.
- [ ] A double-spend attempt is rejected during consensus or validation.

## Notes
This NFR covers safety and finality only; audit evidence and proof delivery remain separate requirements.
